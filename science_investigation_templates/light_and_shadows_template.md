# Light and Shadows Investigation Template

## Investigation Title: Light and Shadows

### Grade Level:
- **Beginner:** Pre-K to 1st Grade
- **Intermediate:** 2nd to 3rd Grade
- **Advanced:** 4th to 5th Grade

## General Objective:
Students will explore the properties of light and shadows through hands-on investigations.

---

## Beginner Level:
### Investigation Questions:
1. What happens when you place your hand in front of a light source?
2. Can you make different shapes with your hands in the light? What do you notice about the shadows?

### Materials Needed:
- Flashlight
- Objects to cast shadows (toys, hands)

### Activities:
1. Shine the flashlight on your hand. What do you see?
2. Try using different objects. Which creates the darkest shadow?

### Reflection:
- What did you discover about shadows?

---

## Intermediate Level:
### Investigation Questions:
1. How does the distance from the light source affect the size of the shadow?
2. How do different objects change the shadows that are created?

### Materials Needed:
- Lamp or flashlight
- Various objects (ball, book, toy)
- Measuring tape

### Activities:
1. Measure the distance from the light source to the object, then measure the shadow.
2. Change distances and observe changes in shadow size.

### Reflection:
- What patterns did you notice?

---

## Advanced Level:
### Investigation Questions:
1. How does the angle of a light source affect the shape of shadows?
2. Can you create a shadow that changes over time? How?

### Materials Needed:
- Protractor
- Flashlight or sunlight
- Various objects

### Activities:
1. Use the protractor to set different angles for the light source and record the shadow shapes.
2. Create a time-lapse of shadows moving during the day.

### Reflection:
- What conclusions can you draw about light and shadows?
